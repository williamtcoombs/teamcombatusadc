Core concepts

Building blocks you’ll feel in every round, drill, and position.

Base & Balance

Stable feet/hips; head over hips. If they can’t move you, they can’t beat you.

Posture & Frames

Protect your spine. Build frames with bone, not muscle.

Connection & Pressure

Stick to what you control, drive through strong lines, remove slack.

Wedges & Levers

Trap joints, isolate segments, use angles—never brute force.

Movement Patterns

Hip escape, bridge, technical stand‑up, knee slide, pummel—rehearse daily.

Breathing & Pace

Stay calm. Breathe through frames. Choose when to surge.

Self‑Defense: awareness to exit

An ounce of prevention beats a pound of cure. We prioritize avoidance, de‑escalation, proportional force, and safe exits.

  • Awareness & boundaries: scan entrances/exits, keep a reactionary gap, phones down.
  • Verbal skills: calm tone, clear statements (“I don’t want trouble”), hands visible at chest level.
  • Hands‑up frame: palms out, elbows in, chin tucked—non‑aggressive posture that protects your head.
  • If you fall: breakfall, frame, technical stand‑up, leave.
