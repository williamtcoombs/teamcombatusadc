Self‑Defense

“An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure.” — Benjamin Franklin

Note: This page is educational and principle‑based. Context and local laws vary. Use only proportional force, prioritize escape, and seek training with a qualified coach.

Awareness & Avoidance

Baseline & Boundaries

  • Scan entrances/exits, lighting, and crowd flow.
  • Keep a reactionary gap; don’t let strangers inside arm’s reach.
  • Carry yourself with purpose; phones down in transitional spaces.

Verbal De‑escalation

  • Calm tone; clear boundaries: “I don’t want trouble. Please back up.”
  • Hands visible at chest level — ready to frame without looking aggressive.
  • Angle your body; move toward exits and witnesses.

Standing: Hands Safe & Disengage

Hands‑Up Frame

Palms out at chest level, elbows in, chin tucked. Create a physical boundary that looks non‑aggressive but protects your head and allows quick movement.

Disengage Path

Move offline, maintain eyes on the person, and back toward light, people, or exits. Avoid going to the ground if there may be multiple threats or hazards.

Grabs & Holds: Simple Releases

Wrist/Clothing Grabs

Rotate toward the thumb opening, step back to create slack, frame at the shoulder/forearm, and disengage. Prioritize breaking contact over counter‑attacks.

Bear Hugs & Clinch

Lower base, protect head/neck, insert frames at hips/face line, turn to an exit lane. If lifted, hook legs to block movement and return to ground with a base.

If You Fall: Protect, Stand, Leave

Breakfall & Frames

Tuck chin, slap to disperse force, create knee/elbow frames to protect head and regain space. Keep feet between you and the threat.

Technical Stand‑Up

Post on hand and opposite foot, protect your face with the other hand, kick stand the rear leg under, and exit while facing the person.

Special Considerations

Multiple Threats / Environment

Keep moving; don’t get tied up. Avoid going to the ground. Use barriers (cars, benches) and head toward lighted areas or open doors.

Legal & Aftercare

Call emergency services when safe. Document details, seek medical attention, and contact authorities. Know local laws regarding self‑defense and reporting.

Train the essentials in a safe room.
