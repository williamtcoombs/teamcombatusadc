The Warrior Ethos

“Fate whispered to the warrior, ‘You cannot withstand the storm.’ The warrior replied, ‘I am the storm.’” — Unknown

The annals of martial arts are filled with warriors. Some are famous—Bruce Lee. Some are architects of whole disciplines—Kanō Jigorō, Masahiko Kimura. Some live in legend—Mulan, the “last samurai.” One shaped each of our lives.

As the art evolves, and as Professor Vogt would demand, so will we. Today and every day that follows, we seek to be worthy of his guidance.

Mindset Pillars

Simple, pressure‑tested ideas that hold up when it matters most.

Awareness First

Read space, intent, and exits. Prevention beats recovery.

Aggressive Simplicity

Gross‑motor, high‑percentage actions delivered decisively.

Position Before Submission

Control lines of force and posture; finish when it’s safe.

Relentless Adaptability

Change levels, angles, and plans without hesitation.

Team Before Ego

Sharpen each other. Win clean, lose with lessons, return better.

Calm Under Fire

Breathe. Frame. Move with intent when others freeze.

Our Ethos

  • 1

    Show up. Consistency beats talent. Put the rounds in.

  • 2

    Protect your partner. Control power. Clear intent. Tap fast, release faster.

  • 3

    Seek pressure. Train where you fail. Build skill where it’s hardest.

  • 4

    Respect the craft. Clean mats, sharp uniforms, coachable minds.

  • 5

    Carry it out of the gym. Courage, humility, and service don’t end at the door.

Code of Conduct

Safety

  • Communicate injuries and limits before the round.
  • No uncontrolled slams or spikes. Respect taps.
  • Use appropriate protective gear for drills.

Culture

  • Help new students. We were all new once.
  • Be on time. Be coachable. Be accountable.
  • Leave the room better than you found it.

On Lineage & Gratitude

We honor those who built the path we walk. We train to carry their standards forward, not to rest on their names. In our room, legacy is lived: through discipline, honesty, and service.

To Professor Vogt—thank you for the pressure, the precision, and the example.

Train the Way You Fight

Live rounds when earned. Technical rounds when required. Always purposeful.

Simple Beats Fancy

Under pressure, the body recalls what it repeats. We repeat fundamentals.
