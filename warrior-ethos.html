The Warrior Ethos

“Fate whispered to the warrior, ‘You cannot withstand the storm.’ The warrior replied, ‘I am the storm.’” — Unknown

The annals of martial arts are filled with warriors. Some are famous—Bruce Lee. Some are architects of whole disciplines—Kanō Jigorō, Masahiko Kimura. Some live in legend—Mulan, the “last samurai.” One shaped each of our lives.

As the art evolves, and as Professor Vogt would demand, so will we. Today and every day that follows, we seek to be worthy of his guidance.

Mindset Pillars

Simple, pressure-tested ideas that hold up when it matters most.

  • Awareness First — Read space, intent, and exits. Prevention beats recovery.
  • Aggressive Simplicity — Gross-motor, high-percentage actions delivered decisively.
  • Position Before Submission — Control lines of force and posture; finish when it’s safe.
  • Relentless Adaptability — Change levels, angles, and plans without hesitation.
  • Team Before Ego — Sharpen each other. Win clean, lose with lessons, return better.
  • Calm Under Fire — Breathe. Frame. Move with intent when others freeze.

Our Ethos

  1. We train with humility, always respecting those who came before us.
  2. We seek mastery through consistent practice and honest feedback.
  3. We value discipline, safety, and the pursuit of excellence.
  4. We honor our teammates by showing up and helping them grow.
  5. We protect the integrity of the art by preserving its traditions while evolving responsibly.
