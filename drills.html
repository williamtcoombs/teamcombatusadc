Drills

"I fear not the (wo)man who has practiced 10,000 kicks once, but I fear the (wo)man who has practiced one kick 10,000 times." — Bruce Lee

The following drills are core to Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu development. They build essential movement patterns, timing, and positional awareness—giving practitioners a strong foundation for all aspects of grappling. These drills help improve guard retention, escapes, transitions, and fluidity both with and without resistance.

Useful Drills

1. Hip Escapes (Shrimping)

One of the most fundamental movements in BJJ. Used in escaping side control, mount, and re-guarding. Teaches how to create space using the hips and maintain frames.

2. Bridging & Rolling (Upa)

Core movement for mount escapes. Builds explosive bridging power, coordination, and directional control. Helps you learn how to off-balance and reverse opponents.

3. Granby Rolls / Shoulder Rolls

Essential for guard recovery, inversion, and flowing through pressure. Enhances body awareness and teaches you to escape and reset when under attack.

4. Technical Stand-Up

Teaches how to stand safely while maintaining a defensive base. Important in both sport BJJ and self-defense. Builds balance and connection to the ground.

5. Guard Retention Drills

Includes wall drills, hip heists, shin circles, and leg pummeling. Develops the ability to keep your opponent from passing your guard with dynamic leg movement and angles.

6. Toreando Pass Drill (Partnered)

Develops timing and reaction for both passing and guard recovery. Helps you practice distance control, footwork, and countering the bullfighter-style pass.

7. Positional Sparring

Live drilling from a specific position (e.g., mount escape, back control). Improves timing, decision-making, and defensive reaction under realistic resistance.

8. Flow Rolling

Cooperative rolling at low intensity to build smooth transitions and awareness. Helps develop sensitivity, reaction, and technique chaining without pressure.

9. Solo Movement Chains

Combine basic movements like shrimp → bridge → stand-up → sit-through. Builds coordination, cardio, and muscle memory for transitions.
