Gameplan

"A goal without a plan is just a wish." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

Rules of Engagement

Self‑Defense

  • De‑escalate, break contact, escape. Avoid the ground when multiple threats exist.
  • Protect head/neck; use frames and technical stand‑up to disengage.
  • Legal/ethical: use only proportional force. Call for help.

Sport

  • Win positions first: takedown/sweep → pass → mount/back.
  • Balance risk vs time/score. Hunt high‑percentage finishes.
  • Manage pace; stay out of the ref’s bad side — work to advance.

Strategy Pillars

First, Don’t Get Held

Fight grips and head position. If they can’t hold you, they can’t throw you or submit you.

Win the Inside

Inside ties, underhooks, and head‑position dominate entries and guard work.

Position → Pressure → Finish

Advance positions, compress slack, isolate, then finish. Skip steps only if forced.

Phase Map

A default path with simple branches. Rehearse until automatic.

Standing → Takedown

  1. Fight for inside ties and head position; clear their strong grip.
  2. Entry: snap‑down to front headlock or level‑change to double/single.
  3. Finish to top: sprawl if shot on; otherwise run the pipe, trip, or mat return.

Top → Pass

  1. Break posture and grips; force their knees together.
  2. Choose a pass family: knee slice, body‑lock, or stack.
  3. Settle side control; kill hips before chasing mount/back.

Side Control → Mount/Back

  1. Crossface + underhook; isolate near arm with a wedge.
  2. Step to mount or float to back as they turn.
  3. Heavy hips; stabilize before submissions.

Back Control → Finish

  1. Head over head. Hooks or body triangle; seatbelt tight.
  2. Two‑on‑one hand fight; trap their arm with legs when possible.
  3. Slide to rear‑naked choke; reset control between attempts.

Bottom Game: Guard Map

If you’re underneath: break posture, control distance, create angles, then stand or sweep.

Closed → Open → Stand/Sweep

  • Break posture with collar/sleeve or two‑on‑one.
  • Open to knee shield or collar‑drag angle.
  • Technical stand‑up to disengage, or off‑balance into a sweep.

Half Guard (Bottom)

  • Frame + underhook to get on a hip.
  • Come up on single leg or take the back as they whizzer.
  • Regard if flattened; no flat back time.

Quick Decision Tree

  • They grab first? Break grip → win inside → snap‑down or level‑change.
  • You’re stuck in guard (top)? Posture, hands safe → stand or knee slice → settle side control.
  • You’re flattened bottom? Frame + hip escape → knee shield → underhook to stand.
  • They turtle? Seatbelt → insert hook → roll with hips → hand fight → choke.

Common Scenarios

Vs. Bigger/Stronger Opponent

Win inside position; avoid collar ties. Angle off, attack legs, and use body‑lock passes to remove frames.

Vs. Wrestler/Heavy Top

Early frames; deny crossface. Build to knee shield, threaten sit‑ups to stand or take single.

Street Context

Break contact when possible. Technical stand‑up and leave. Protect head/hands; scan for others.

Points Match

Secure early lead with takedown/sweep and pass. Stabilize, then hunt the back for high‑value control.
