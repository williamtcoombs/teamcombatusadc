Competition

"Every champion was once a contender who refused to give up." — Rocky Balboa

Competing in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu requires not only technical skill but also strategic thinking, emotional control, and rule-set mastery. These principles guide athletes in preparing for and performing during competition at any level—from local tournaments to high-level championships.

Competition Principles

1. Have a Game Plan

Enter each match with a structured strategy: preferred takedown or guard pull, passing route, submission chain, and defensive reactions. Your success improves when your game plan is clear and rehearsed.

2. Time and Score Awareness

Track the clock and scoreboard throughout the match. Adjust your urgency and risk level based on whether you're ahead or behind. This awareness often determines victory in close matches.

3. Positional Control Wins Matches

Secure dominant positions before going for submissions. Holding mount, back control, or side control solidly scores points and often sets up high-percentage finishes.

4. Risk Management

Don’t take unnecessary risks. Avoid wild scrambles or sloppy attacks—especially if you're ahead. Be methodical and protect your base and balance.

5. Transition Over Stagnation

Always look to improve position or apply pressure. Referees favor active grapplers, and staying stagnant invites resets or point deductions.

6. Play to the Rule Set

Understand the rules of your event (IBJJF, ADCC, EBI, etc.). Tailor your strategy to maximize points and avoid penalties or disqualifications. Rule awareness is a hidden advantage.

7. Conditioning & Recovery

Matches can be intense and physically demanding. Train with elevated pace, simulate tournament conditions, and prioritize recovery between rounds and events.

8. Emotional Control

Stay composed during high-pressure moments. Whether winning or losing, emotional discipline lets you make smart decisions and avoid mental collapse.

9. First Score Advantage

The first points often dictate the match pace. Scoring first puts pressure on your opponent and lets you dictate the tempo and strategy going forward.
